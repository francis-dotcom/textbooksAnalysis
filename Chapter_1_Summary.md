# Chapter 2: International Flow of Funds - Key Concepts

Based on the PowerPoint presentation, here are the most important aspects:

## 1. **Valuation Models**
   - **Domestic Model** - Traditional single-country valuation
   - **Multi-National Model** - Expanded valuation framework for international operations

## 2. **Corporate Governance in International Context**
   - Agency problems in multinational corporations
   - Differences in governance structures across countries

## 3. **Foreign Exchange Risk**
   - **Exchange Rate Volatility** - Example: Monthly percentage changes in Japanese Yen-U.S. Dollar exchange rate
   - Impact on international business operations and cash flows

## 4. **Cultural Differences**
   - How cultural factors affect international business decisions
   - Cross-cultural management challenges

## 5. **Political Risk**
   - Government instability and policy changes
   - Impact on international investments and operations

## 6. **Market Imperfections**
   - **Case Study: Nestlé's Market Imperfection**
     - Daily prices of Nestlé's Bearer and Registered Shares
     - Example of how market inefficiencies create opportunities

## 7. **Expanded Opportunity Set**
   - Benefits of international diversification
   - Access to global markets and resources

## 8. **Goals for International Financial Management**
   - Maximizing shareholder value in a global context
   - Managing international risks and opportunities

## 9. **International Businesses & Multinational Corporations (MNCs)**
   - Definition and characteristics of MNCs
   - Common finance decisions in international business:
     - Capital budgeting across borders
     - Financing decisions in multiple currencies
     - Working capital management internationally
     - Risk management strategies

## Key Takeaways:
- International finance involves managing additional complexities beyond domestic finance
- Exchange rate risk is a fundamental challenge
- Market imperfections can create both risks and opportunities
- Cultural and political factors significantly impact financial decisions
- MNCs must balance global opportunities with local risks
